Getting Started

How to Use This Guide

This plan is designed to work with your body's natural hormonal rhythm, not against it. It follows the framework popularized by Dr. Mindy Pelz in her work on fasting and the female cycle.

Where Do You Start?

Day 1 = the first day of your period. Figure out approximately where you are in your cycle right now and start on that day. For example, if you're on day 14 of your cycle, start at Day 14 in this plan. Then, when your next period begins, restart the plan from Day 1. From that point on, you'll be fully synced.

Phase 1 · Days 1–20
Standard Phase

During menstruation and the follicular phase, your body responds really well to intermittent fasting. Your estrogen is rising, insulin sensitivity is higher, and your metabolism is running efficiently. You're in your fat-burning sweet spot.

You'll eat 2 meals + 1 snack + 2 adrenal cocktails per day, keeping your eating window to roughly 8 hours.

2 meals · 1 snack · 2 adrenal cocktails
Phase 2 · Days 21–30
Luteal Phase

As progesterone rises in the second half of your cycle, so does your hunger — and that's completely normal. Your body is preparing for the possibility of pregnancy and needs more consistent fuel.

Fighting this hunger leads to cravings, bingeing, and falling off track. Instead, we lean into it: you'll eat 3 meals + 1 snack + 2 adrenal cocktails per day. More frequent eating keeps blood sugar stable and progesterone supported.

3 meals · 1 snack · 2 adrenal cocktails
Why This Matters

Most diet plans ignore your cycle entirely — which is why so many women feel like they "fail" every month around the same time. You're not failing. You're fighting your biology. This plan works with it instead.

The Numbers

Understanding Your Calories

This plan is built around approximately 1,500 calories per day. That's a caloric deficit for most women — meaning you'll be eating less than your body burns, which is what drives fat loss.

This is not a forever number

1,500 calories is a tool for fat loss, not a lifestyle. Once you reach your goals, you should increase to your maintenance calories. Think of this as a phase, not a permanent way of eating.

How to Find Your Number

Search for a TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) calculator online. Enter your height, weight, age, and activity level. That number is roughly what your body burns each day at your current size.

To lose about 1 pound per week, subtract 500 calories from your TDEE. That's your personal deficit target — and for many women, it lands right around 1,500 calories, which is why this plan uses that as the starting point.

If your deficit target is higher than 1,500 cal

Use this plan as a base and add to it. Extra protein, more vegetables, or slightly larger portions are all good options. Don't skip meals just to stay at 1,500 if your body needs more.

If your deficit target is lower than 1,500 cal

Be careful here. Going too low can hurt your metabolism, your hormones, and your energy levels. For most women, 1,200 calories should be the absolute floor — and even that should only be temporary. Please don't push lower than that.

The Bottom Line

Use your TDEE as your guide. This plan gives you the structure — your numbers tell you whether to follow it as written or adjust the portions to fit your body.

The Strategy

Why High Protein + High Fiber

Every meal in this plan is built around two pillars: plenty of protein and plenty of fiber. Here's why both matter so much when you're eating in a deficit.

Protein · ~135g / day

When you eat in a caloric deficit, your body doesn't just burn fat — it can break down muscle too. That's the opposite of what you want. Losing muscle slows your metabolism, changes how your body looks, and makes it harder to keep the weight off.

Eating high protein tells your body to preserve muscle and burn fat instead. This is critical for your metabolism (muscle burns more calories than fat, even at rest) and for how you look and feel as you lose weight. Every meal in this plan is built around a protein-rich foundation.

Fiber · 30–40g / day

Fiber is the unsung hero of fat loss. Most people don't talk about it enough. Here's what it actually does for you:

  • 1

    Keeps you full

    When you're eating fewer calories, hunger is the number one reason people quit. Fiber slows digestion and keeps you feeling satisfied for hours. This is arguably the most important benefit when you're in a deficit.

  • 2

    Supports your hormones

    Fiber helps your body process and eliminate excess estrogen. When estrogen builds up, it can cause bloating, mood swings, and weight gain — especially around the midsection.

  • 3

    Feeds your gut

    A healthy gut microbiome affects everything from your mood to your metabolism. Fiber feeds the beneficial bacteria that keep your digestive system running smoothly.

Your Adrenal Cocktail

You'll see two adrenal cocktails built into every day of this plan. Here's everything you need to know about what they are, why they matter, and how to make them.

What Is It?

A simple, nourishing drink designed to support your adrenal glands — the tiny powerhouses responsible for your stress response, energy levels, and hormonal balance.

Why It Matters

When you're in a caloric deficit, your body can interpret the reduced food intake as a form of stress. Your adrenal glands may start working overtime, which can lead to fatigue, cravings, and hormonal disruption. The adrenal cocktail provides a targeted blend of minerals and nutrients to keep them supported so that doesn't happen.

When to Drink It

One at 10:00 AM and one at 2:00 PM. These are the times your cortisol naturally dips during the day — and the cocktail helps smooth out those energy valleys so you don't hit a wall.
